# CardArch Marketing Strategy

## Positioning

CardArch is a simple browser-based business card maker where users can create, customize, save, and export professional front/back business cards quickly.

Main product strengths:

- Front and back business card design
- Editable templates
- Logo upload
- Auto color palette generation from logo colors
- Saved projects that can be edited later
- PNG, JPG, and PDF export

Suggested positioning:

> Create professional business cards from your logo in minutes.

## Target Users

Start with a narrow audience instead of marketing to everyone.

Best early audiences:

- Freelancers
- Small business owners
- Print shops
- Designers who need fast drafts
- Startup founders
- Students making personal cards
- Local service businesses such as salons, repair shops, tutors, and real estate agents

## Content Strategy

Use product-building content. Show the app improving over time.

### 1. Feature Updates

Example:

> New update: You can now upload your company logo and auto-generate matching card colors from it.

### 2. Before/After Demos

Show the full transformation:

- Blank card
- Logo uploaded
- Palette generated
- Final exported card

### 3. Short Tutorials

Example:

> How to create a business card in 30 seconds.

### 4. Build-in-Public Posts

Example:

> Today I improved the saved projects screen so users can edit old card designs later.

## Best Platforms

Start with these platforms:

- LinkedIn: best for business and product updates
- X/Twitter: good for build-in-public updates
- TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts: best for quick visual demos
- Facebook groups: useful for local small business audiences
- Reddit and Product Hunt: better after the product is more polished

## Weekly Posting Plan

Simple posting schedule:

- Monday: product update
- Wednesday: short demo video or GIF
- Friday: before/after design post
- Sunday: feedback post or next-feature teaser

Example posts:

- I added logo upload to my business card maker.
- Now the app suggests colors based on your logo.
- You can save projects and edit them later.
- Which template should I add next?

## Soft Launch Strategy

Do a soft launch before a public launch.

1. Share the app with friends, designers, freelancers, and small business owners.
2. Ask 10 people to create a card.
3. Watch where they struggle.
4. Fix the top 3 issues.
5. Launch publicly after the main workflow feels smooth.

## Public Launch Checklist

Before launching publicly, prepare:

- Clear homepage or landing section
- 3 to 5 strong template examples
- Short demo video
- Working PNG, JPG, and PDF export
- Acceptable mobile layout
- Feedback or contact option
- Optional "Made with CardArch" branding

## Update Sharing Format

Use this format for regular product update posts:

```text
New CardArch update:

Added [feature].

Now users can:
- Benefit 1
- Benefit 2
- Benefit 3

Demo below.
```

Example:

```text
New CardArch update:

I added auto color palette generation from uploaded logos.

Now users can upload a logo and instantly get matching card colors for their business card design.

Next: more templates and better export options.
```

## Growth Ideas

Strong product-led growth ideas:

- Shareable preview links
- More templates by industry
- Duplicate project option
- Print-ready PDF with bleed
- Watermark-free paid export
- QR code customization
- Template categories such as Modern, Minimal, Corporate, and Creative
- "Create from logo" guided flow

## Monetization Plan

Start free, then add paid features later.

### Free Features

- Basic templates
- PNG export
- Limited saved projects

### Paid Features

- PDF export
- Print-ready bleed support
- Premium templates
- Unlimited saved projects
- Custom QR codes
- Remove watermark
- Brand kit and saved logo palettes

## Main Principle

Post progress before the product is perfect. Regular updates build trust, show momentum, and give people a reason to follow the product.
